What is SEO? Your Guide to Ranking on Google

Published on September 13, 2025 by Udith Babu K N

This guide will demystify Search Engine Optimization (SEO) for beginners. It starts with a clear, concise definition: SEO is the process of improving the quality and quantity of website traffic from unpaid, or "organic," search engine results.

The article will then explain the three fundamental pillars of SEO, using simple analogies and examples to ensure clarity for everyone.

The Three Pillars of SEO

  • On-Page SEO: This pillar involves optimizing the content and HTML source code of a page on your website. Think of it as making sure your webpage speaks the same language as the search engines. This includes using relevant keywords in your titles and headings, writing high-quality content that answers the user's questions, and optimizing your images.
  • Off-Page SEO: This refers to actions taken outside of your own website to impact your rankings within search engine results pages. It's largely about building your website's authority and reputation. The primary method is earning backlinks—links from other reputable websites to yours. Think of these backlinks as votes of confidence from other sites.
  • Technical SEO: This pillar focuses on optimizing your website's infrastructure to help search engine crawlers find, understand, and index your site more effectively. This includes ensuring your site is fast, mobile-friendly, and has a clear structure (like a sitemap) for search engines to follow.
